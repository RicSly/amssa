African Maritime Safety and Security Agency. AMSSA

The African Maritime Safety and Security Agency provides administrative support for all African Nations, its mantra is to Protect the African Maritime Legacy for Future Generations. Its purpose is to procure, centralise, and analyse data. And to provide access to services, innovate technology and proven knowledge, freely available or at optimum cost for African States, Governments and their Agencies, National Maritime Administrations, Navies, Coastguards, Law Enforcement Agencies, Border Control and Ports.

AMSSA core goals are based on a consensus of value, common interests and common strategic objectives and we are guided by the fundamental principles of the unity of Africa, the interdependence between Africa and the global community, ownership and joint responsibility, respect for human rights, democratic principles and the rule of law.

Our Services

At AMSSA, we offer a wide range of services aimed at enhancing maritime safety and security across African nations. Our solutions are designed to meet the specific needs of National Maritime Administrations, Navies, Coastguards, and Maritime Law Enforcement bodies.

Data Procurement

We specialize in the procurement of maritime data, ensuring that vital information is gathered and made freely available for the benefit of African States and Governments.

Discover More

Technology Centralization

Our agency centralizes cutting-edge maritime technology to empower African nations in safeguarding their maritime interests and securing their borders.

Knowledge Analysis

We analyze maritime knowledge to provide actionable insights for African States, Governments, and their agencies, enabling informed decision-making and strategic planning.

Capacity Building

AMSSA is committed to enhancing the capacity of African nations in maritime safety and security through training, skill development, and knowledge transfer programs.

Collaborative Partnerships

We facilitate collaborative partnerships between African nations, international organizations, and maritime stakeholders to promote collective efforts in ensuring maritime safety and security.
